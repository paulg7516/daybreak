Brand exploration

Daybreak logo explorations

Every direction from this round, designed at icon, wordmark, favicon, and dark-tile sizes. Grouped by idea. The app-tile style is the modern, sharp direction; open-form marks are the calmer alternative.

Dawn & sunrise marks

Daybreak

App tile + sun Current

Navy app icon with a white rising-sun knockout. The modern app-mark idiom; what is live on the site now.

Daybreak

Gradient tile + sun Bolder

Same tile with a blue-to-purple gradient. More pop, more premium - but adds the gradient you were unsure about.

Daybreak

Rising sun + reflection

Open-form: a sun on the horizon with a reflection line. Calmer, less "app icon." The pre-tile version.

Daybreak

Sun over lanes

Sun atop two stacked bars - fuses dawn with the triage board. Gets a little muddy at favicon size.

Daybreak

Concentric arcs

Sun core with rising arcs. Dynamic, but reads close to a rainbow / gauge at small sizes.

Daybreak

Half-disc + horizon

The simplest dawn mark. Clean, but the dome can read as a hill / loaf rather than a sun.

Product-concept marks (no sunrise)

Daybreak

Focus list Top non-sun pick

A feed with the top item active and the rest dimmed - "what needs you, first." Product-true and distinct from a plain list.

Daybreak

Funnel

The pile, filtered down to what matters. Clear and modern, but funnel is a common/generic filter glyph.

Daybreak

Split "break"

A disc broken by a band of light - the day-break wordplay. Clever, but can read as a half / contrast toggle.

Daybreak

Checkmark

"Caught up." Friendly and legible, but it is the to-do-app cliche - least distinctive.

Daybreak

Priority lanes Clashes w/ Nowtify

Stacked bars with the top lane emphasized. Strong concept, but too close to Nowtify's stacked-bar mark - ruled out.

Monogram

Monogram "D"

Bold letter tile. The safest, most scalable mark - but concept-light (it is just a letter).

Sorting, filtering & routing

Daybreak

Triage board Sorting pick

Columns of a board with varying content. Daybreak literally is a board, so it fits - and stays distinct from Nowtify.

Daybreak

Route

One stream split into lanes - exactly what declared intent does. Most ownable, but can read as a git-branch / share glyph.

Daybreak

Sort

Arrow + graduated bars. Instantly legible as "sort," but it is a standard UI control glyph - a little generic.

Daybreak

Filter sliders

Tune what comes through. Clean and modern, but reads as the universal "settings / filter" glyph.

Daybreak

Layers

Organized into stacked layers. Modern, but the layers glyph is widely used and concept-loose.

Daybreak

Inbox Off-message

A tray, caught up. Reads clearly, but the site says Daybreak is "not another inbox," so this fights the positioning.

Fragmented → whole  Day · Break, put back together

Daybreak

Reassembled disc Concept pick

Four shards with clean seams locking into one circle. Best tells the story - broken, then made whole. Reads abstractly as a sun without being literal.

Daybreak

Reassembled disc - 3 shards

Same idea with three pieces. Fewer seams, so it reads less like a pie chart and a touch more deliberate.

Daybreak

Assembling

The same shards pulled a few pixels apart - caught mid snap-together. More energy and motion; slightly busier small.

Daybreak

Split & aligned

Two halves, broken then set back together with a small offset. Cleanest and calmest, but the reassembly story is subtle.

Daybreak

Vertical fracture

A disc split down the middle, halves shifted. The seam is more obvious than the horizontal split.

Daybreak

Fault line

One whole disc with a fracture running through it. Honest "break," but the crack can read as a lightning bolt.
