How it works

The sender decides. You just triage.

No guessing at what matters from subject lines. Importance is declared at the source, then sorted for you.

1

Senders tag at the source

In Outlook, a colleague marks what they need from you and an optional deadline. The tag travels with the message.

RespondApproveReviewFYI
2

Daybreak gathers what is addressed to you

Tagged mail plus your Jira Service Management tickets. Untagged noise never enters the board, and everything is processed locally.

3

You come back to lanes, not a pile

Items sort by action type. Urgency badges float Overdue and Today to the top. "Catch up since" defaults to your last visit.

Features

A companion board, not another inbox

Daybreak only holds what was deliberately routed to you. That is the whole point.

Declared intent, no guessing

The sender's tag is the lane. No recipient-side inference, no scoring you have to second-guess.

Four clear lanes

Respond, Approve, Review, FYI. Every item lands in exactly one, so you always know the next action.

Urgency at a glance

Overdue, Today and This week badges, derived from sender deadlines and ticket SLAs.

Email and JSM together

Outlook and Exchange mail beside your Jira Service Management tickets. Gmail read-only too.

Local-first and private

Your mail and tickets are processed on your machine. Nothing about your queue lives on someone else's server.

Built for catching up

"Catch up since" dates, keyboard triage, bulk-clear and honest "you're all caught up" empty states.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Does it read my whole inbox?
No. Daybreak only ingests messages a sender deliberately tagged for you, plus the Jira Service Management tickets assigned to you. Everything untagged stays out of the board.
Where does my data live?
On your machine. Daybreak is local-first by design. It connects to your mail and ticket accounts to fetch what is tagged, then keeps and processes it locally.
What does it connect to?
Outlook and Exchange email, Gmail as a read-only source, and Jira Service Management for your assigned tickets.
How do people tag a message?
With the Daybreak Outlook add-in. The sender picks a lane from one dropdown, optionally adds a deadline, and sends as usual. No extra steps for you.
Which platforms are supported?
macOS and Windows. The download above gives you the right build for your platform.
